How We Work

Our culture is built on four principles that show up in everything from product reviews to performance conversations: ship quickly, listen actively, write things down, and protect the player.

Ship Quickly

We believe that working software in production teaches us more than any internal review. Our teams operate in short cycles, ship features behind feature flags, and roll out gradually based on real usage data. Most engineers will see their first production deploy within their first week.

Listen Actively

Player feedback is the heartbeat of the company. Every product team has a direct feedback loop with support and a rotating responsibility to read raw player conversations. The product roadmap is shaped as much by what players ask for as by what we plan internally.

Write Things Down

We are a documentation-first company. Decisions are recorded in lightweight RFCs. Postmortems are blameless and shared widely. We default to async, written communication, which makes us inclusive of teammates in different time zones and produces a durable record of how the company thinks.

Protect the Player

Every product decision starts with the question: 'How does this help or harm the player?' We've killed promising features that would have been confusing for players. We've delayed launches to add responsible-gaming safeguards. This isn't an external compliance posture — it's how we want to operate.

How We Hire

Our hiring process is designed to be respectful of your time and to give you a real sense of what working at Raxiwin is like. Most processes consist of four steps and complete within two to three weeks.

We start with a 30-minute call with a recruiter to understand what you're looking for and to share more about the role. Strong fits move on to a technical or craft-based screen tailored to the role — for engineers this is typically a short, take-home exercise that respects your weekend; for product and design roles it is a structured portfolio walk-through. The next stage is a series of conversations with future teammates focused on collaboration, problem solving, and values fit. We finish with a final discussion with the hiring manager and, if all goes well, an offer.

Throughout the process we commit to clear communication, honest feedback, and decisions within five business days of every stage. We know that interviewing is stressful, and we want to make our part of it as painless as possible.

A Day in the Life

What does a typical day at Raxiwin actually look like? Naturally it varies by role, but a few things are common across the company. Mornings tend to start with asynchronous catch-up — overnight progress from teammates in different time zones, written updates from each squad, and decisions to review or unblock. Mid-mornings are when most synchronous work happens: design reviews, planning sessions, technical discussions. Afternoons are for deep work, with a strong cultural norm against scheduling meetings after 14:00 local time wherever possible.

Wednesdays are 'no internal meetings' days across the entire company. This protected space exists so that everyone — engineers, designers, support specialists, finance — has at least one day a week to focus deeply on the work that requires it.

Fridays end with a company-wide demo session, open to anyone who wants to show what they have built or learned that week. Demos are short, casual, and unfiltered. They are one of our favourite traditions and the single biggest reason newer employees feel a sense of belonging quickly.

Growth and Development

We believe that careers are built, not assigned. Every employee works with their manager to define a personal growth plan covering the skills they want to develop, the projects that would help them get there, and the support they need from the company. Plans are reviewed quarterly and adjusted as priorities and interests evolve.

Our annual learning budget can be spent on conferences, courses, certifications, or books — entirely at the employee's discretion. Many of our most senior engineers came up through this kind of self-directed learning, and we deliberately preserve that path for the next generation.

Internal mobility is actively encouraged. Around a third of our promotions each year involve a change of team or function, often into roles the employee could not have imagined when they joined. If you want to move from support into product, or from engineering into trust and safety, or from marketing into partnerships — we will help you make it happen.

Compensation Philosophy

We pay competitively at every level, benchmarked against the top decile of comparable roles at major global tech companies. Compensation has three components: a base salary, a meaningful equity grant, and an annual bonus tied to a combination of company and individual results. We publish internal salary bands for every role and level, so every employee can see exactly where they sit and what would change with a promotion.

Bands are reviewed annually against external market data, and we make broad-based adjustments when the market moves. We do not negotiate pay; everyone at the same level and location is paid within the same band. This approach is simpler, fairer, and removes one of the most persistent sources of pay inequity.

Inclusion in Practice

Building a diverse team is something we take seriously, and we measure ourselves on outcomes rather than intentions. We track representation at every level of the company by gender, geography, and a number of other dimensions where employees voluntarily share data, and we publish a summary of these metrics annually.

Concretely, our hiring processes use structured interviews with predefined evaluation rubrics; we anonymize CVs at first screen for most roles; we ensure that interview panels are diverse; we offer dedicated mentorship and sponsorship programmes for employees from under-represented groups; and we provide meaningful, equal parental leave for primary caregivers of any gender.

Working at a Real-Money Company

Raxiwin handles real player funds and operates in a regulated industry. That reality shapes how we work in important ways. Every employee, regardless of role, completes mandatory training in responsible gaming, anti-money-laundering, and data protection. Engineers working on financial systems are subject to a higher standard of code review and change management. Support staff are trained to recognize at-risk players and empowered to escalate any concern.

We mention this because it is the kind of thing that is hard to appreciate from the outside but defines a lot of what daily work feels like. If you are excited by the intersection of consumer product and serious financial responsibility, you will find a lot to love. If you would rather work somewhere where mistakes have no real consequences — that's fine too, but it isn't us.

Engineering Interview Process

If you are interviewing for an engineering role at Raxiwin, here is exactly what to expect so there are no surprises.

Stage one is a short conversation with one of our recruiters, focused on understanding your background, your interests, and what you are looking for in your next role. We will also share more about the team you would be joining and the projects currently in flight.

Stage two is a take-home exercise designed to be completable in approximately three hours of focused work. We are deliberate about the scope to respect your time. The exercise is a realistic miniature of work you would actually do on the job, and you are welcome to use any tools, references, or AI assistants you would normally use. We are evaluating how you think, not whether you can perform under artificial constraints.

Stage three is a series of three to four interviews with future teammates, spread across one or two days based on your availability. The interviews cover technical depth in your area of expertise, system design at the appropriate level for the role, collaboration and communication, and a values-based conversation focused on the kind of teammate you would be.

Stage four is a final conversation with the hiring manager, focused on aligning on the role, the team, and the kind of impact you would have. After the final stage we commit to a decision within five business days. Offers are made directly by the hiring manager.

Product and Design Interview Process

The product and design interview process follows a similar shape but with different content. After the recruiter conversation, the next stage is a portfolio walk-through where you talk us through two or three projects you have shipped, focusing on the problem you were solving, the decisions you made, and what you learned. This is not a presentation — it is a structured discussion, and we are most interested in how you think.

Subsequent stages include a product or design exercise tailored to the role, conversations with cross-functional partners (engineering, support, data), and a final conversation with the hiring manager. The full process typically completes within two to three weeks.

Working Hours and Time Zones

Most teams at Raxiwin operate asynchronously with a small overlap window each day for synchronous collaboration. The exact overlap depends on the team composition and is negotiated by each team. We are deliberate about preserving deep work time and about respecting the rhythms of teammates in different time zones.

On-call rotations exist for engineering and support teams that operate critical services. Rotations are paid, equitably distributed, and structured to avoid sustained sleep disruption. We track on-call load carefully and reduce it where possible through investment in reliability and tooling.

A few recent examples: a senior support agent who became a responsible-gaming specialist after demonstrating exceptional skill in handling sensitive conversations. A finance analyst who moved into payments engineering after teaching themselves SQL and rebuilding the team's reporting dashboards. That kind of leadership is the standard we expect from every manager at Raxiwin, and it is one of the strongest signals we look for when promoting people into management roles.

Remote Work Done Well

Remote work is not new at Raxiwin — we have been remote-first since founding. Over the years we have learned a lot about what makes remote work succeed and what makes it fail.

The biggest single factor in success is high-quality written communication. Decisions get recorded. Context gets shared. Anyone joining the conversation late can catch up by reading rather than by interrupting. We invest heavily in writing skills, and many of our most senior individual contributors are also some of the best writers in the company.

Other success factors include structured weekly one-on-ones, deliberate effort to build personal relationships across time zones, occasional in-person off-sites that include genuinely social time (not just sessions), and explicit support for the practical realities of working from home — including a home-office stipend, ergonomic equipment, and clear norms around working hours.

More About Working at Raxiwin

We close this page with a few additional notes for prospective applicants — the kind of detail that often does not fit in a job description but matters a great deal when deciding whether a company is right for you.

On feedback culture: we take feedback seriously, in both directions. Every manager runs a weekly one-on-one with each direct report, and every employee runs a quarterly upward-feedback survey on their manager. Feedback is treated as a professional skill that takes practice; we train extensively in how to give it and how to receive it. The single biggest reason long-tenured employees give for staying at Raxiwin is the quality of feedback they receive from their managers and peers.

On work-life balance: we are a serious company doing serious work, but we are not a sweatshop. Sustained overtime is treated as a failure of planning, not a badge of honor. Most people work close to a standard week, take their vacation, and have lives outside of work. When crunches do happen — and in any operationally critical business they sometimes do — they are time-bounded and followed by explicit recovery time.

On compensation transparency: we publish internal salary bands for every role and level, alongside the criteria used to determine which band an individual sits in. New hires are placed in bands using the same criteria, and offers are not negotiable on base salary. This approach is simpler and fairer than the alternative, even though it occasionally costs us hires who value the negotiation process itself.

On parental leave: primary caregivers of any gender receive 16 weeks of fully paid leave. Secondary caregivers receive 4 weeks. Returning parents can choose a phased return-to-work schedule that suits their family situation. We have built our staffing model to absorb parental leave gracefully and we explicitly do not want returning parents to feel that their absence has cost them career momentum.

On disability accommodation: we work proactively with employees and candidates to remove barriers wherever they exist. We have made significant investments in accessibility of our internal tools and our hiring process, and we continue to expand this work as we learn from the experience of our team members. If you require any accommodation during the application process or in the role itself, please tell us — we will figure it out together.

On the kind of person who thrives here: we have noticed that the people who stay the longest and grow the fastest at Raxiwin share a few characteristics. They are curious — genuinely interested in how things work and why. They are kind — not necessarily soft, but consistently respectful of colleagues and players. They are accountable — they own their work, including their mistakes, without drama. And they are calm — they handle pressure without amplifying it for the people around them.

If those characteristics describe you, we would love to hear from you whether or not you see an obvious match in the role list above. The future of Raxiwin will be shaped by the people we hire over the next few years, and we are genuinely excited to find them.
