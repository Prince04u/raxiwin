Commercial Models in Detail

Choosing the right commercial model matters enormously to your business, so here is more detail on each of the structures we offer.

Revenue share gives you a percentage of the net gaming revenue generated by players you refer, paid for the lifetime of those players' activity. Our standard rate starts at 35% and scales upward based on monthly net revenue. This model rewards long-term player quality and is well suited to partners who can produce content with enduring shelf life.

Cost per acquisition (CPA) gives you a fixed payment for each qualifying first-time depositor referred. The qualifying threshold is set on a per-deal basis and typically requires a minimum deposit and a minimum amount of post-deposit activity. CPA suits partners running paid campaigns where predictable, near-term economics matter more than long-term tail.

Hybrid combines a CPA payment for the initial conversion with a reduced revenue share thereafter. This structure rewards both successful acquisition and player quality, and is often the most attractive option for partners with both scale and audience loyalty.

Channel-Specific Guidance

Different traffic channels have different rules and best practices. Here is a brief overview of what works well — and what to avoid — on each.

Search-driven content sites generally perform best when they focus on genuinely helpful information: honest reviews, in-depth guides, and content that helps players make informed choices. Thin content that exists solely to capture brand searches tends to underperform and may attract penalties from search engines.

Video and live-streaming platforms reward authenticity above almost everything else. Audiences quickly tune out creators who appear to be reading from a script or playing for the camera. The best-performing video partners simply play the games they enjoy, with honest commentary, and let their natural enthusiasm do the work.

Email and direct messaging channels are highly effective when used with permission-based, well-segmented lists. They are not effective and not permitted when used for unsolicited messaging.

Paid social and search require careful compliance attention because policies vary by platform and country. Our partner managers can advise on what is currently permitted and on best practices for staying compliant.

Content Ideas That Convert

Some content formats consistently outperform others in our affiliate channels. Comprehensive game reviews, written after the reviewer has actually played the game for several hours, perform extremely well. So do honest comparisons of bonus offers across operators, payment-method guides for specific regions, and beginner-focused explainers on how particular game types work.

Underperforming formats include generic 'best casino' listicles, copy-pasted bonus terms, and articles that fail to disclose their affiliate nature. Audiences are sophisticated; they reward honesty and ignore the obviously generic.

Programme Policies and Best Practices

The Raxiwin Affiliate Programme is governed by a set of policies designed to keep the partnership fair, sustainable, and aligned with the broader responsibilities of operating in a regulated industry. The most important policies are summarized below; the full versions are available in the partner portal.

Attribution and cookie policy: clicks are attributed using a combination of first-party cookies and server-to-server tracking. The default cookie window is 30 days for registration and lifetime for revenue share once a player has registered through your link. Where multiple partners refer the same player, the most recent qualifying click takes attribution unless a different rule has been specifically negotiated.

Commission calculation: revenue share is calculated on net gaming revenue, which is gross gaming revenue less bonuses, chargebacks, payment processing fees, and applicable gaming taxes. CPA is paid on qualifying first-time depositors meeting the deal-specific minimum deposit and activity thresholds. Calculations are run on the first business day of each month; payments are issued within seven business days thereafter.

Negative-balance carry-over: standard agreements do not carry negative balances forward from one month to the next, meaning that you start each month with a clean slate. Some custom deal structures include carry-over provisions; these are always negotiated explicitly and disclosed clearly in the deal terms.

Trademark and intellectual property: partners are licensed to use the Raxiwin name and approved logos solely for the purposes of promoting the platform under the programme. Partners must not register Raxiwin trademarks in any jurisdiction, must not bid on Raxiwin brand keywords in paid search without explicit written permission, and must not register domain names that incorporate Raxiwin trademarks or close variations thereof.

Prohibited traffic sources: traffic from sources such as malware, spyware, browser hijackers, incentivized clicks, unsolicited email, automated bots, and click-fraud rings is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate termination, forfeiture of unpaid commissions, and possible legal action.

Responsible gambling messaging: all promotional content must include appropriate age-restriction notices and responsible-gambling messaging in line with the requirements of the markets being targeted. Templates for compliant messaging are available in the partner portal.

Player protection commitments: partners may not target self-excluded players, players known to be under the legal gambling age, or individuals identified as being at risk of harm. Partners with access to player-level data are required to handle that data in accordance with applicable data protection law and our specific partner data-handling guidance.

Termination and consequences: either party may terminate the agreement on 30 days' notice. Termination for cause — including serious breach of these policies — is effective immediately and may result in forfeiture of unpaid commissions, recovery of previously paid commissions on fraudulent activity, and exclusion from future participation in the programme.

Dispute resolution: most disagreements are resolved quickly through direct discussion with your affiliate manager. For matters that cannot be resolved at that level, the programme operates a formal escalation process documented in the partner portal, culminating in arbitration where necessary.

Sub-affiliate programme rules: partners participating in the sub-affiliate programme are responsible for the compliance of partners they refer. Repeated compliance failures by referred partners may result in adjustment of sub-affiliate commission terms.

Programme updates: programme terms may be updated from time to time. Material updates are communicated to active partners with at least 30 days' notice. Continued participation in the programme after the effective date of an update constitutes acceptance of the updated terms.
