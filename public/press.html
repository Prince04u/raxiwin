Our Approach to Media Engagement

Raxiwin operates in an industry that attracts strong opinions, and we welcome that. We believe a healthy gambling industry is one that is scrutinized by journalists, debated in public, and held to account when it falls short. Background on Online Gaming in 2026

The online gaming industry in 2026 is significantly more mature, more regulated, and more technologically sophisticated than it was even five years ago. Total global online gaming revenue is estimated at well over one hundred billion US dollars and continues to grow at a healthy single-digit annual rate, with mobile representing the dominant access channel in essentially every major market.

Three structural trends shape the current environment. The first is the steady professionalization of the operator industry: a smaller number of larger, better-capitalized operators now account for the majority of the regulated market, with smaller specialists serving particular niches well. The second is the increasing role of regulators in setting baseline standards for player protection, advertising, and integrity. The third is the continuing migration of payments toward faster, more transparent rails, particularly cryptocurrencies and instant fiat alternatives.

Within this environment, Raxiwin occupies a position as a mid-large operator with particular strengths in payments speed, customer support, and responsible-gaming product. We are growing faster than the market average in our priority regions and are investing accordingly in product, support, and compliance capacity.

Our Stance on Industry Issues

We are happy to be on the record on a number of industry topics where journalists often look for operator perspective.

On advertising standards: we support stricter, more consistent rules across markets and oppose the use of imagery, language, or placement that could appeal to minors. We have voluntarily adopted standards more conservative than legally required in several jurisdictions we operate in.

On affordability checks: we recognize the goal of preventing players from gambling beyond their means, while also recognizing the practical and privacy challenges of intrusive checks. We favor approaches that combine behavioral analytics with light-touch verification, escalating to deeper checks only where genuine risk indicators exist.

On crypto: we view cryptocurrency as a legitimate, useful payment innovation that can co-exist with traditional rails. We oppose the framing of crypto as inherently illicit, while also supporting strong anti-money-laundering controls applied consistently across all payment methods.

On taxation: we accept that gaming is a legitimately taxed activity and pay all taxes due in every jurisdiction we operate in. We support efforts to create more harmonized, internationally consistent tax frameworks.

Background: How Raxiwin Approaches Operating in a Regulated Industry

A recurring theme in journalist conversations is the question of how operators reconcile commercial growth with the responsibilities of operating in a regulated industry. This section sets out Raxiwin's approach in some depth, in the hope that it provides useful context for stories that touch on industry conduct.

Our starting point is that responsible operation is not a constraint on commercial success but a precondition for it. A business that loses the trust of regulators, players, or the public cannot sustain growth, regardless of how attractive its short-term economics may look. We have therefore made significant investments in the disciplines that earn and maintain trust: compliance, responsible gaming, anti-money-laundering controls, complaint handling, and external transparency.

Concretely, compliance and responsible-gaming roles report independently of revenue-generating functions. The teams have explicit authority to block product launches, marketing campaigns, and operational decisions that would breach internal standards, and they exercise that authority regularly. The Chief Compliance Officer has direct access to the board on any material issue.

Our responsible-gaming product investment exceeds the regulatory minimum in every jurisdiction we operate in. We have voluntarily adopted standards stricter than legally required on issues including the design of session reminders, the cooling-off period on limit increases, the prominence of self-exclusion options, and the use of behavioral analytics to identify at-risk players. We see these investments as competitive differentiators, not costs.

On anti-money-laundering, we operate a risk-based programme calibrated to the specific patterns we see across crypto and fiat rails. Our compliance team includes specialists with prior experience at major banks and at supervisory authorities. We invest continuously in transaction monitoring, source-of-funds verification, and ongoing customer due diligence. Suspicious activity is reported to relevant authorities in accordance with applicable law.

On marketing standards, we apply consistent rules across our own activity and across our affiliate partners. We do not target marketing at self-excluded players, minors, or individuals identified as being at risk. We screen affiliate creative before it goes live. We do not buy advertising in placements likely to be seen primarily by minors. Where regulations restrict the use of celebrity endorsement or specific types of imagery, we apply those restrictions globally rather than only in the relevant markets.

On complaints, we publish substantive responses within 14 days for all formal complaints and aim to do so within 7 days for the majority. We participate in alternative dispute resolution where available. We treat complaint volume and resolution time as a leading indicator of product and service health and incorporate them into our internal reporting alongside financial metrics.

On transparency, our annual transparency report covers operational metrics, player-protection metrics, complaint statistics, and a summary of the most significant compliance issues identified and addressed during the year. We share more detail with regulators and recognized researchers on request.

On regulatory engagement, we participate actively in industry working groups on responsible gaming, payments, and standards-setting. We support efforts to harmonize regulation across jurisdictions where harmonization improves player outcomes. We engage constructively with consultations and provide evidence-based feedback to regulators on proposed rule changes.

We recognize that the public reputation of the broader online gambling industry has been damaged by the conduct of operators that have prioritized short-term revenue over their obligations to players and to the wider system. Restoring that reputation is, in part, the responsibility of operators willing to demonstrate a different approach. We are committed to being one of those operators.

Journalists working on stories that touch on these issues are warmly invited to engage with us. We will give you serious, honest answers on the record, and we will share whatever evidence we can to support claims we make. We expect to be held to account for what we say, and we welcome that.

Final Notes for Members of the Media

We close this press room with a short statement of intent. The online gaming industry sits at the intersection of several genuinely important public-interest issues: consumer protection, financial regulation, data privacy, technological innovation, and the broader social consequences of how entertainment is monetized in the digital age. Serious journalism on these issues makes the whole industry better, and we want to be useful to the journalists pursuing it.

We will not always agree with the framing of every story. We will sometimes wish a specific quote had been included or a specific nuance preserved. That is the nature of editorial independence and we accept it without complaint. What we ask is that journalists who cover us reach out for our perspective before publishing, give us a fair opportunity to respond to specific factual claims, and represent our positions accurately whether or not they agree with them. We will reciprocate by being responsive, substantive, and on the record.

We are happy to engage on positive stories, critical stories, and everything in between. We are particularly happy to engage on stories that go beyond the surface — stories that examine how a specific feature was designed, how a specific player-protection intervention works in practice, how a specific category of fraud is detected, or how a specific regulatory issue plays out across markets. The more substantive the inquiry, the more we have to offer.

If you have read this far, you are exactly the kind of journalist we want to work with. Please reach out at press@raxiwin.com whenever there is a story we can help with, and we will be in touch quickly.
