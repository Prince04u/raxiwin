Our Story

Raxiwin was founded in 2017 by a team of veteran engineers, product designers, and gaming-industry professionals who shared a common frustration: existing online casinos were slow, opaque, and frequently unfair. Withdrawals took days. Bonus terms were buried in unreadable legal text. Customer support was outsourced to call centers that didn't speak the player's language. We knew we could do better.

In the early days, our office was a single rented room in Bengaluru with three desks, four monitors, and one overworked espresso machine. We launched our first prototype with a handful of slots and a single payment provider, and within a month we had over 10,000 sign-ups — entirely through word of mouth. That early traction taught us something important: players are hungry for honesty in this industry.

Over the years we have grown enormously. Today, Raxiwin offers over 5,000 games from more than 70 software studios, supports more than 30 cryptocurrencies and fiat methods, and serves players in 12 languages. But the core philosophy hasn't changed. We're still a small team at heart, still obsessed with details, and still building the kind of platform we ourselves would want to use.

Our Operating Principles

Behind every product decision at Raxiwin sits a set of principles that we revisit constantly and refuse to compromise on. These aren't slogans printed on office walls — they're the questions we ask each other in design reviews, in incident postmortems, and in every weekly all-hands meeting.

The first principle is that the player is never the enemy. In too many corners of the gambling industry, operators view their customers as adversaries whose money must be extracted as efficiently as possible. We reject that framing entirely. A long-term, healthy relationship with a happy player is worth more to us than any short-term squeeze, and we structure our incentives, our bonuses, and our support culture around that belief.

The second principle is that transparency is a feature, not a marketing tactic. We publish our RTP figures, we publish our uptime numbers, we publish our average withdrawal processing times, and we let players verify every in-house game result for themselves. When something goes wrong — and in any complex system, things do sometimes go wrong — we tell players what happened, why it happened, and what we did to fix it.

The third principle is that simple is hard but worth it. It is far easier to ship a cluttered cashier with eighteen confusing options than to design a calm, focused flow that does the right thing by default. We invest the extra design effort because complexity in product translates directly into anxiety for players, and anxious players make worse decisions.

How We Measure Success

Most casinos measure themselves on a single metric: gross gaming revenue. We track that number too, but we put it deliberately in the context of other numbers that matter just as much. We track the percentage of players who use at least one responsible-gaming tool. We track the median time to resolve a support conversation. We track the share of withdrawals processed within our published service-level targets. We track player retention twelve months after registration, not just twelve days.

Each of these numbers is reported to the executive team weekly and to the entire company monthly. When a metric moves in the wrong direction, we treat it as seriously as a revenue miss. This combination of commercial and player-welfare metrics keeps us honest about the kind of business we want to build.

Our Engineering Culture in Depth

If you peer past the polished interface that players see every day, the most distinctive thing about Raxiwin is its engineering culture. We have invested years in building a codebase that is calm rather than clever, observable rather than mysterious, and resilient rather than merely functional. Every system that touches player money goes through a separate, stricter review process than the rest of the codebase, and every critical service has at least two engineers who can confidently maintain it end to end.

We deploy to production hundreds of times each week. The vast majority of those deploys are small, incremental changes shipped behind feature flags and rolled out gradually based on real telemetry. This continuous deployment cadence is not a vanity metric — it is what allows us to fix issues within minutes of identifying them, and to iterate on player feedback in days rather than quarters.

Our incident response process is documented, rehearsed, and explicitly blameless. When something goes wrong we focus relentlessly on the systemic conditions that allowed the failure rather than on individual mistakes. Every significant incident generates a written postmortem with concrete action items, all of which are tracked to completion. Senior leadership reviews postmortem trends quarterly to ensure we are learning from them rather than repeating them.

Why We Built Raxiwin Originals

Even with thousands of third-party games on the platform, we decided early to invest in a small, focused in-house studio called Raxiwin Originals. The reason was simple: we wanted at least one part of the catalogue where we controlled every detail, from the math model to the visual treatment to the post-launch tuning.

Raxiwin Originals titles are built around three constraints that we believe define a great modern game. They must be provably fair, with verification scripts that any player can run. They must be readable on a small screen without sacrificing depth on a larger one. And they must be honest about volatility — a player who looks at the game info should know exactly what kind of session they are signing up for.

The studio is deliberately small, currently a team of fourteen, and we plan to keep it small. We would rather ship four exceptional games a year than forty mediocre ones. Player feedback on Originals titles has been some of the most positive we have ever received, and we treat that response as both validation and responsibility.

Looking After the Money

A casino is, fundamentally, a business that holds other people's money. We take this responsibility extremely seriously. Player funds are held in segregated bank and crypto accounts, separated from the company's operating capital and protected from creditor claims in any insolvency scenario. The adequacy of these segregated reserves is audited by an external firm on an annual basis, and a summary of the audit findings is published on this site.

Day-to-day, our treasury team monitors balances continuously to ensure that liquidity is always sufficient to meet expected withdrawals even in periods of unusually high volume. We maintain pre-arranged contingency facilities with multiple banking and crypto-liquidity partners so that even a sudden surge in withdrawal volume can be met without delay. We have never failed to meet a withdrawal obligation on time, and we have engineered our processes specifically to ensure we never do.

Where We're Going Next

We don't share detailed product roadmaps publicly, both because they change frequently in response to player feedback and because we believe it is more honest to ship things than to announce them. That said, a few broad themes are likely to define the next year of work at Raxiwin.

First, payments. We will keep pushing the boundary of how quickly money moves through our system. We are investing in additional crypto rails, in faster fiat alternatives in our priority markets, and in better tooling for players who want to manage multiple payment methods seamlessly. Five-minute withdrawals are no longer enough; one-minute withdrawals are within reach.

Second, personalization done responsibly. Modern players expect a product that feels tailored to them — but tailoring in a real-money entertainment context comes with serious ethical responsibilities. We are building personalization that helps players find games they will genuinely enjoy, without ever using personalization to encourage at-risk patterns of play.

Third, social and community features. Gaming is more fun when shared, and we have only scratched the surface of what is possible in this regard. Expect tournaments, leaderboards, and community-driven events that bring players together in healthy, opt-in ways.

Finally, deeper localization. We have great coverage in our core markets, but there are players in many other regions whose first-class experience we have yet to build. Localization is more than translation — it is payment methods, customer support, marketing tone, and an understanding of regulatory context. We will keep doing the patient work this requires.
